Symmetry AD in MRI

Mohammad A. N. Al-Azawi (2021)

  • Brain tumor/anomalies classification and segmentation can be of 3 types:
    • Manual segmentation
    • Semi-automatic segmentation; initialisation, feedback response, evaluation
    • Fully automated segmentation
  • Challenges: for CV diagnosis on brain regions
    • Differences in shapes in patients’ brains. To which I can add differences in lesions.
    • Image segmentation: tumor types, different brightness levels and imaging conditions
    • Features extraction difficulties because of the natural brain shape
  • Therefore: not possible to predict tumor status in someone’s brain based from other MRIs, hence the symmetry (left, right) suggestion.
  • Tumor identification:
    • detection (this paper’s focus)
    • location, isolation
    • measurement
  • Symmetry between 2 lobes determine if abnonaly or not.
  • Brain abnormality identification:
    • Non-ai:
      • Thresholding
      • Clustering (k-mean)
      • Edge-based (not fully automated)
