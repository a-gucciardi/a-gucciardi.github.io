DL in infant MRI

2019 - M.Mostapha, M.Styner

  • Ressources for segmented MRIs given.

  • Focus on the isointense age: 5-9 months. Higher potential for successful translation to other time points including the neonatal stage.

  • Atlas-base segmentation techniques are challenged by registration errors in low contrast and intensity inhomogeneity from the infant population.

  • Probabilistic methods and deformable-based models require large labelled datasets.

  • Longitudinal approaches: require follow-up scans.

  • Anatomy-guided tissue segmentation and toplogical correction framework: adopt a signed distance map with respect to the outer cortical surface as anatomical prior knowledge, and incorporate such prior information into the proposed framework to guide segmentation in ambiguous region. link

    • relies on engineered features

  • CNN, patch based-CNNs, Fully CN, Dilated C, UNet like(+ pre-training), Other CNNs

  • DL for early disease prediction in infants:

    • Early interventions -> improved prognosis.
    • Relies on studies that are disease-specific, or at least group level differences-brain behavior correlations. Many studies used ML to classify indiv with NDDs particularly autism.
    • Lack of studies bc of various infants classification challenges
    • Review of DL applied to ASD prediction
    • Conv networks on surface
    • Conv via 2D surface mapping
    • Multi-task learning for dimensional prediction

  • Open Challenges in infant MRI:

    • Data size, ie. required dataset size depends on the expected variability seen in the studied process.
      • Reduce the need for additional training data by careful ML framework design, like smaller filters for deeper layers - different architecture grouping - hyperparamter optimization.
      • Transfer learning (careful on 2D pre-trained NN which ignore anatomical context orthogonal to the 2d plane). Solution papers cited.
      • Data augmentation, but with limits (heterogeneity of data)
    • Class imbalance, training examples skewed towards typical. (NDD % prevalence)
      • Typical ML inblance fix not applicable on DL on medical complexes app.
      • various solutions presented (Over/under sampling, 1class learning, hybrids)
    • Interpretation:
      • Sample-specific (saliency map), improv directions
      • Diagnostic role for the NN leading to better architecture

  • Discrimitative models generalize well only when labeled data is plentiful. Generative Models which can exploit labeled and unlabeled can be a solution. BUT traditional GM have shown inability to scale up to high dim datasets.

    • Solution and discussion on how generative models can be used. VAEs GANs + variations
      • They work precisely on GANs abilities
