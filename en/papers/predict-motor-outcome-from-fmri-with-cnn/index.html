Motor outcome prediction

2020 - S.Saha et al.

  • Motor outcome measurement with existing assessment.
  • ~30 weeks PMA subjects.
  • Uses 3D patches of MRI as input, 26x26x26 after gridsearch selection. Only take patches containing >50% brain matter. Used registration for skullstriping.
  • Data augmentation: left-right flip, because any anatomical variation in the right hemisphere benefits the left and respectively.
  • Model architecture, classification CNN: 3x [[2DCNN]] into 3 [[FC]].
    • A complete image was classified as abnormal if at least 27% of its patches predicted abnormal outcome. Scan-based predictions.
    • Didnt try [[3DCNN]] for cost reasons but suggest it.
  • Heatmaps generated to identify locations associated with outcomes.
    • Average of the heatmap over the crossvalidations.
  • Results do not involve any a-priori knowledge of MRI extraction.
  • The patch-based technique could leverage of the localised outcome prediction, ie. recognize the brain area associated with adverse outcome.
    • Some locations findings were indeed confirmed by biolological knowledge. But clinical relevance still need further investigations. This is very NDD specific and depends on the type of assessment too.
    • As of 2020, ‘one patch in one decision’ strategies were never used for MRI neurodevelopmental outcome prediction.
  • Predict outcomes from the scans, applicable to other conditions where localised lesions are expected.
